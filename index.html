P. Diddy

According to Wikipedia, Sean John Combs (born November 4, 1969),[4] also known by his stage names Puff Daddy, P. Diddy and Diddy, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, actor, record producer and entrepreneur. He was born in Harlem and was raised in Mount Vernon, New York. He worked as a talent director at Uptown Records before founding his label Bad Boy Entertainment in 1993.

Bad Boy Entertainment (also known as Bad Boy Records) has a been a renowned record label since it's beginning in 1993. P. Diddy, creator and also artist under the record label, began his start in the music industry as a non-paying intern for Andre Harrell - founder of Uptown Records. After being fired by Harrell, Diddy created the now famous Bad Boy Records. The label's first release was Craig Mack's famous "Flava in Ear" which would later be remixed by one of Bad Boy Entertainment's biggest artist, The Notorious B.I.G. Bad Boy Entertainment had a roster of names now known around the world.
